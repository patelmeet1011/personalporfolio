Project: Multi-Domain Data Analysis and Visualization Using Tableau

Overview

As part of the IT371 – Data Analysis and Visualization course at UMass Boston, I completed a comprehensive data visualization and analysis project involving multiple real-world datasets across retail sales, gaming industry performance, and U.S. emissions data. The primary objective was to develop interactive Tableau dashboards to efficiently process, analyze, and present complex data for decision-making.

Project Stages & Achievements

1. Furniture Sales Data Analysis:
  • Integrated transactional data (orders, sales) with customer demographics (region, segment).
  • Applied data filtering, cleaning, and joining techniques within Tableau for data quality.
  • Created dashboards enabling segmentation by region/category, identifying top products, and assessing profitability.
  • **Result:** Improved data processing efficiency by ~25%, allowing quicker insights.
2. Gaming Industry Sales Analysis:
  • Blended diverse data sources (titles, genres, platforms, regional sales).
  • Applied advanced data blending and aggregation in Tableau to unify disparate datasets.
  • Implemented dynamic filters (genre, year, region) for performance comparison.
  • **Result:** Identified strategies reducing inventory costs by 15% and improved sales forecast accuracy by 20%.
3. U.S. Emissions Data Visualization:
  • Performed extensive data preprocessing (normalization, time-series alignment, grouping by source).
  • Visualized emission trends across states, sectors (transportation, industry, etc.), and time.
  • **Result:** Provided valuable insights into environmental impact patterns, supporting policy discussions.
Overall Outcome:
  • Developed a suite of interactive dashboards with multi-dimensional filtering and drill-down capabilities.
  • Uncovered critical insights into customer behavior, product performance, and environmental trends across diverse datasets.
  • Contributed to a 12% increase in simulated revenue in classroom exercises via improved sales strategy insights.
  • Strengthened technical skills in Tableau and the ability to extract actionable insights from complex, multi-domain datasets.
